## Picasso's Prolific Philosophy

"Inspiration exists, but it has to find you working."

### The Math of Genius
- 50,000 works over 75 years
- 667 works per year
- Nearly 2 works per day, every day
- Painted until the day before he died at 91

### Picasso's Daily Practice
- Wake: ~11 AM (night owl)
- First session: 2 PM - 10 PM (8 hours)
- Dinner break: 10 PM - 11 PM
- Second session: 11 PM - 2-3 AM (3-4 hours)
- Total: 11-12 hours of creative work daily

### The Core Principles

1. **Work Precedes Inspiration**
   - Don't wait to feel creative
   - Sit down and work
   - Inspiration arrives while working, not before

2. **Quantity Enables Quality**
   - Most work will be mediocre
   - Gems emerge from volume
   - You cannot predict which pieces will be great
   - The only way to find gems is to produce many stones

3. **Consistency Over Intensity**
   - Daily practice beats occasional marathons
   - Show up every day
   - The habit becomes stronger than resistance

4. **Accept Failure**
   - Most pieces will be forgettable
   - Failure is the cost of finding success
   - Each failure teaches something for the next attempt

5. **Never Linger**
   - Finish and move on
   - The current work is never as interesting as the next work
   - Overworking destroys freshness

## Output System Design

### The Non-Negotiable Daily Minimum
A creative quantity so small it's impossible to skip:
- Writers: 100 words minimum
- Visual artists: One sketch minimum
- Musicians: 10 minutes of creation minimum

This is the floor, not the goal. Most days you'll exceed it. But the floor guarantees consistency.

### The Ambitious Target
Where you're actually aiming:
- Writers: 1,000-2,000 words per session
- Visual artists: 3-5 complete pieces per day
- Musicians: One complete draft per day

Aim high. Miss often. Still produce more than you did before.

### The Session Structure
1. **Start ritual** (5-10 minutes): Signal to your brain that creation begins
2. **First block** (60-90 minutes): Primary production time
3. **Rest** (10-15 minutes): Complete break
4. **Second block** (60-90 minutes): Continue or start new piece
5. **End ritual** (5 minutes): Review what was produced (no judgment), note next step

### The Quality Ban
During production:
- No judging while creating
- No editing while drafting
- No refining while generating
- Quality assessment comes later, in separate sessions
- The production session is sacred space for output only

## Prolific Output System

### Current State
- Domain: Songwriting
- Current output: ~12 songs/year
- Available time: 2 hours/day (14 hours/week)
- Primary block: Perfectionism, over-refinement, waiting for the "right" idea

### Your Picasso Numbers
**Lifetime goal:** 3,000 songs (you have perhaps 40 creative years—75 songs/year)
**Annual target:** 75 songs (more than 6x your current output)
**Monthly target:** 6-7 songs
**Weekly target:** 1-2 complete song drafts
**Daily minimum:** One verse OR one chorus OR one melodic idea (5-10 minutes of actual creation)
**Daily ambitious target:** Complete rough draft of one song

### Daily Schedule Design

**Start time:** 7:00 PM (home from work, short break, then create)
**Session 1:** 7:00 PM - 8:00 PM (60 minutes - main creation)
**Break:** 8:00 PM - 8:10 PM (10 minutes - step away completely)
**Session 2:** 8:10 PM - 9:00 PM (50 minutes - continue or start new)
**Total daily production time:** 1 hour 50 minutes

### The Rituals

**Start ritual:**
1. Put phone in another room
2. Open instrument/DAW without reviewing old work
3. Set timer for 60 minutes
4. Start with whatever comes—no filtering

**End ritual:**
1. Save/record whatever exists—no judgment
2. Write one sentence: "Tomorrow I could try..."
3. Close everything
4. Do not listen back until designated review day (Sunday)

### Perfectionism Intervention
1. **No editing during creation sessions.** Drafts are drafts. Refinement happens in separate, time-boxed sessions (max 2 hours per song, ever).
2. **First draft = done draft.** A song that exists beats a song that's perfect in your head.
3. **The 30-Minute Rule:** If you've spent more than 30 minutes on any single element (lyric line, chord, etc.), move on. Come back in a future session with fresh ears.
4. **Weekly "garbage song":** One song per week must be written in under 30 minutes. This trains your brain that fast is possible.

### The Mediocrity Acceptance Statement
"Most of my songs will be forgettable. That is the cost of writing the few that matter. I give myself permission to write 60 mediocre songs this year so that I can find 15 good ones. The ratio is not the problem—the ratio is the method."

### Tracking System
- Simple tally: number of complete drafts per week
- No quality scoring—that comes later
- Weekly count visible on wall/phone
- Monthly review: only counting, never judging

| Wed | 1 melody | Full draft | |

### Picasso's Command
"You have written 12 songs this year. I wrote 2 works per day for 75 years. The difference is not talent—it is showing up. You wait for inspiration; I worked until inspiration found me. Sit down. Write a bad song. Then write another. The good songs are hiding inside the pile of bad ones. The only way to find them is to make the pile."
