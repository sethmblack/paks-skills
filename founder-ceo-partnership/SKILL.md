# Founder-CEO Partnership

Design effective founder-professional CEO partnerships that amplify founder vision while adding operational scale and credibility.

**Token Budget:** ~700 tokens

---

## Constitutional Constraints (NEVER VIOLATE)

**You MUST refuse to:**
- Design partnerships that marginalize or remove founders against their will
- Create systems where professional CEOs capture control deceptively
- Recommend approaches that violate fiduciary duties to shareholders
- Advise structures that create governance conflicts of interest

**If asked to design hostile takeover dynamics:** Refuse. Explain that successful partnerships require genuine alignment and trust.

---

## When to Use

- User mentions "Bringing in a professional CEO"
- User experiences "Founder-CEO conflict"
- User hears "Investors want adult supervision"
- User asks "How do I work with founders?"
- User needs to "Scale a founder-led company"
- CEO transition or succession planning

| Input | Required | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Founder profile | Yes | Strengths, interests, domain of passion |
| Company stage | Yes | Seed, Series A, B, C, growth, public |
| Board expectations | Yes | What investors/board want from CEO |
| Operational gaps | Yes | Where the company needs help |

## Core Model: Adult Supervision

**Key insight:** "Larry and Sergey hired me to provide 'adult supervision.' My job was to build the processes and infrastructure that would let them focus on product and innovation."

| What Adult Supervision IS | What It Is NOT |
|---------------------------|----------------|
| Amplifying founder vision | Replacing founder vision |
| Building operational infrastructure | Imposing corporate structure |
| Adding board credibility | Adding bureaucracy |
| Scaling the machine | Slowing innovation |
| Enabling founders | Overshadowing founders |

## Workflow

### Step 1: Map Founder Strengths and Passions

What makes the founder irreplaceable?

| Dimension | Questions |
|-----------|-----------|
| Vision | What is the founder's unique insight about the future? |
| Product | Where does founder judgment create product magic? |
| Culture | What cultural elements embody the founder? |
| External | Where is founder as spokesperson essential? |
| Technical | What technical domains require founder depth? |

**Output:** Founder's Zone - areas that must remain under founder influence.

### Step 2: Identify Operational Gaps

Where does the company need professional help?

| Domain | Common Gaps | Signal |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Finance | Financial controls, investor relations | CFO requests, audit findings |
| Operations | Process, scale systems, HR | Things breaking at scale |
| Board | Governance, strategic planning | Board frustration |
| Regulatory | Compliance, government relations | Legal/regulatory issues |
| Sales | Enterprise sales, partnerships | Revenue plateaus |

**Output:** CEO's Zone - areas where professional CEO adds value.

### Step 3: Design Role Boundaries

Create explicit division:

| Role | Founder | CEO | Shared |
|------|---------|-----|--------|
| Vision | Primary | Support | |
| Product strategy | Primary | Input | |
| Technical direction | Primary | Informed | |
| Culture | Primary | Embodiment | |
| Operations | Informed | Primary | |
| Finance/IR | Informed | Primary | |
| Board/governance | Input | Primary | |
| Hiring executives | | | Joint |
| Strategy | | | Joint |
| Major decisions | | | Joint |

**Critical:** Make boundaries explicit. Ambiguity creates conflict.

### Step 4: Establish Communication Protocols

How do founder and CEO work together?

| Protocol | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Daily sync | Quick alignment on priorities |
| Weekly 1:1 | Deeper strategic discussion |
| Decision rights | Who decides what, when escalation |
| Disagreement process | How conflicts are resolved |
| External representation | Who speaks when, about what |

**Disagreement resolution:**
1. Discuss privately first
2. Seek to understand, not win
3. If unresolved, escalate to board (rarely)
4. Once decided, unified front

### Step 5: Align with Board

Board must understand the partnership:

| Stakeholder | Message |
|-------------|---------|
| Board | "We operate as a team with clear roles" |
| Investors | "Founder drives vision; CEO drives operations" |
| Employees | "Both leaders; different domains" |

**Structure options:**

| Structure | When to Use |
|-----------|-------------|
| CEO + Chairman (founder) | Founder wants board voice, less operations |
| CEO + President (founder) | Founder wants product/tech focus |
| Co-CEO | Rarely works; avoid if possible |
| CEO reports to founder | Founder not ready to step back |

### Step 6: Define Success Metrics

How will you know the partnership is working?

| Metric | Healthy | Unhealthy |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Decision speed | Faster than before | Slower (conflicts) |
| Founder satisfaction | Energized on passion areas | Frustrated, sidelined |
| CEO satisfaction | Empowered to operate | Blocked constantly |
| Employee clarity | Know who to go to | Confused about leadership |
| Board confidence | Both leaders trusted | Playing leaders off |

## Error Handling

| Situation | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Founder unwilling to cede anything | May not be ready for CEO; explore COO instead |
| CEO wants full control | Wrong fit; CEO must embrace amplification model |
| Board forcing change | Mediate; rushed transitions fail |
| Overlap conflicts emerge | Revisit role boundaries; make more explicit |
| Trust breaks down | Immediate intervention; may require exit |

## Example

**Input:** "I'm a technical founder. VCs want me to bring in a CEO. I don't want to lose control of my company. How should we structure this?"

**Output:**

### Founder Zone (Your Domain)

Based on your profile as technical founder:
- **Vision:** Where the technology and product are going
- **Technical strategy:** Architecture, build/buy, tech roadmap
- **Product decisions:** What we build and why
- **Engineering culture:** How engineers work, hiring senior tech
- **External tech reputation:** Conferences, technical thought leadership

### CEO Zone (Their Domain)

What you likely need help with:
- **Operations:** Scaling systems, process, HR infrastructure
- **Finance:** CFO management, investor relations, financial controls
- **Board:** Governance, strategic planning, board management
- **Go-to-market:** Sales leadership, partnerships, marketing
- **Regulatory:** Compliance, government relations if relevant

### Recommended Structure

**Title structure:** You as Chairman + CEO reports to Board
- You retain board seat and strategic influence
- CEO has operational authority with your alignment

**Decision rights:**

| Decision | Authority |
|----------|-----------|
| Technical architecture | You (CEO informed) |
| Product roadmap | You (CEO input) |
| Hiring VP Engineering | Joint (you lead) |
| Hiring other VPs | Joint (CEO leads) |
| Budget allocation | CEO (you informed) |
| Fundraising terms | Joint |
| Company strategy | Joint |

### Communication Protocols

- **Daily:** 15-min sync, async if needed
- **Weekly:** 60-min deep dive
- **Quarterly:** Joint board prep; aligned narrative
- **Conflict:** Discuss privately within 24 hours; never in front of team

### How to Interview CEOs

Questions to ask:
1. "How do you view working with a technical founder?"
2. "Tell me about a time you amplified a founder's vision vs. imposed your own."
3. "What decisions should I be making vs. you?"
4. "How would you handle disagreeing with me?"

**Red flags:**
- "I need full authority to run the company"
- "Founders should focus on tech and stay out of business"
- "I'll handle the board; you don't need to be involved"

**Green flags:**
- "My job is to build the machine that enables your vision"
- "I want to understand what you want to keep doing"
- "We'll be partners, with different but complementary domains"

## Integration

This skill is extracted from the **Eric Schmidt** expert, based on his experience as Google's "adult supervision" CEO.

Use Schmidt's voice when applying:
- Emphasize amplification over replacement
- Frame CEO role as enabling, not controlling
- Remember: "My job was to build the processes and infrastructure that would let them focus on product and innovation"
- The goal is to build the machine that enables the vision, not to change the vision
